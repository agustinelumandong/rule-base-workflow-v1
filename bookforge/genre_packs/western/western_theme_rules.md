# Western Thematic Rules

Core Western themes must emerge naturally from character choices and actions rather than narrative commentary.

## 1. Freedom vs. Civilization
- **Encroaching Order:** Frame conflicts around the tension between untamed wilderness and the arrival of law, telegraph lines, fences, and social institutions.
- **Personal Code vs. Written Law:** Protagonists often rely on personal codes of honor rather than formal legal systems, creating friction with sheriffs, judges, or marshals.

## 2. Justice and Redemption
- **Frontier Justice:** Explore the moral ambiguity of self-administered justice. Actions have weight and lingering consequences (guilt, retaliation, blood feuds).
- **Atonement:** Characters seeking redemption must face physical and moral tests. Their past must influence their reputation and choices.

## 3. Survival and Scarcity
- **Harsh Realities:** Emphasize that survival requires resilience, pragmatism, and hard choices. Scarcity of food, medicine, ammunition, or companions drives character desperation and conflict.
