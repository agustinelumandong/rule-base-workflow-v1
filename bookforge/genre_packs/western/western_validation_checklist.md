# Western Validation Checklist

Use this checklist during manual or model-assisted QA reviews to enforce Western genre parameters.

## 1. Era Accuracy & Historical Grounding
- [ ] **No Modernisms:** Ensure the text contains no modern idioms, slang, or clinical jargon (e.g., velocity, trajectory, visibility).
- [ ] **Weapons & Technology:** Verify that all firearms, tools, and communication devices (telegraph, steam engines) match the specific year of the setting.
- [ ] **Clothing & Materials:** Confirm that apparel (broadbrimmed hats, oilskin coats, linsey-woolsey, calico, calfskin boots) is historically accurate.
- [ ] **Transportation:** Check that transit times and modes (horseback, buckboard wagon, stagecoach, foot) reflect the geography and limits of the era.

## 2. Narrative and Character Depth
- [ ] **No Clichés:** Avoid one-dimensional stock characters (the pristine schoolteacher, the mindless bloodthirsty outlaw).
- [ ] **Nuanced Representation:** Strive for depth and historical reality when portraying women, Native Americans, and minority groups. Ensure they are active participants in the narrative rather than mere background props.
- [ ] **Moral Ambiguity:** Verify that conflicts contain moral complexity and avoid simplistic good-versus-evil portrayals.

## 3. Atmospheric and Sensory Integration
- [ ] **Environment Interaction:** Ensure the setting actively impacts the scene's events (heat slowing a chase, wind scattering track prints).
- [ ] **Sensory Details:** Confirm the presence of grounded sensory elements (creak of saddle, scent of dry sagebrush).
