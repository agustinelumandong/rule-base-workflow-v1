# Western Setting and Atmosphere Rules

The landscape of the American West is an active driver of the plot. Follow these guidelines to build an authentic setting.

## 1. Environmental Forces
- **Scarcity and Hazard:** Ground scenes in environmental challenges. Water sources, trail conditions, heat, dust, and dry wells must affect the characters' physical limits and resources.
- **Scale and Distance:** Convey the vastness of the frontier. Travel between locations must require time, planning, and wear on horses and gear.

## 2. Lived-in Architecture
- **Frontier Structures:** Describe settlements with historical specificity. Use elements like rough-hewn pine boards, false-front storefronts, canvas tents, dusty streets, and sun-bleached shingle roofs.
- **Sensory Details of Towns:** Highlight the sights and smells of a frontier town: the scent of wet mud, horse manure, coal oil lamps, sawdust on saloon floors, and cheap whiskey.
