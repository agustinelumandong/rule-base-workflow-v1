# Western Era Accuracy Rules

Ensure that the material culture and technology described in the prose match the mid-to-late 1800s.

## 1. Weapons & Calibers
- **Era-Correct Arms:** Use weapons appropriate to the decade. A cartridge-firing Colt Single Action Army (1873) or Winchester Model 1873 rifle is perfect for post-Civil War settings, while cap-and-ball percussion revolvers (e.g., Colt 1851 Navy, Remington 1858) fit earlier settings.
- **Physical Handling:** Describe mechanical operation accurately: cocking the hammer, loading cartridges through a gate, cleaning powder fouling, and the smell of sulfur and grease.

## 2. Clothing & Materials
- **Era Textiles:** Use correct fabrics: wool, canvas, calico, linsey-woolsey, stiff denim, and oiled leather.
- **Garment Terms:** Characters wear duster coats, waistcoats, broadbrimmed Stetson or slouch hats, high-heeled riding boots, braces (not suspenders), and bandanas.

## 3. Transportation & Time
- **Realistic Speeds:** A horse walks at ~3-4 mph and trots at ~8 mph. Do not depict long-distance travel at a continuous gallop. Riding 30 miles in a day is a strenuous journey requiring rest and care.
- **Gear Maintenance:** Detail the care of mounts: unsaddling, currying, picking hooves, and checking horse shoes.
