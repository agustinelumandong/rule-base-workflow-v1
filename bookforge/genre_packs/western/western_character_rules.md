# Western Character Rules

Western characters must avoid stock caricatures and carry internal motivations that drive their external conflicts.

## 1. Protagonists & Antagonists
- **Flawed Heroes:** Protagonists should carry physical or psychological scars. Their motivations must be shaped by past choices, obligations, or losses.
- **Motivated Antagonists:** Avoid mustache-twirling villains. Antagonists must have a rational personal goal or code, even if it brings them into direct conflict with the protagonist.

## 2. Nuanced Representation & Cultural Sensitivity
- **Frontier Women:** Frontier women should be portrayed with agency and resilience. Avoid treating them solely as victims or background figures; depict them as ranchers, business owners, homesteaders, or doctors.
- **Native Americans:** Portray Native American characters with individual depth, cultural specificity, and historical realism. Avoid harmful tropes, flat stereotypes, or treating them as plot accessories.
- **Minority Populations:** Reflect the historical diversity of the West, including Black cowboys, Mexican vaqueros, Chinese railroad workers, and European immigrants.
