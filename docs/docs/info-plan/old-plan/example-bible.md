# Title: Lone Star Reckoning

**Genre:** Traditional Western
**Length:** Approximately 40,000 words (20 chapters of roughly 2,000 words each)

## Setting
Shadow Ridge, Texas – a dusty cattle town on the edge of the Llano Estacado, 1876. One main street, a two-story hotel, saloon, bank, livery, small jail, and a scattering of adobe and frame houses. The town sits in a shallow valley with rocky ridges to the north and open prairie to the south. Late summer heat, dust storms, and the constant lowing of longhorns in the distance.

## Hero
**Darin Mayweather**, 38, U.S. Marshal. Tall, lean, sun-browned, with a quiet voice and eyes that have seen too much. Carries a Colt .45 Peacemaker with a worn walnut grip and a Winchester '73. No family left—outlaws killed his wife and daughter six years earlier in the Nations. He works alone because he trusts no one else to finish the job. The governor calls him when regular law has failed.

## Main Antagonists: The Calhoun Gang
- **Rufus "Iron Fist" Calhoun** – 42, leader, built like a blacksmith, sadistic, carries two Navy Colts and a Bowie knife.
- **Vance "Snake" Calhoun** – younger brother, fast with a knife, rattlesnake mean.
- **Jed "Deadeye" Harlan** – sharpshooter, half-blind in one eye, uses a scoped Henry rifle.
- **Bull McGee** – 6'6", 280 lbs, prefers axe handles and fists.
- **Luis "El Diablo" Vargas** – knife fighter and arsonist.
- **Billy "Kid" Thompson** – 19, trigger-happy, wants to make his name.

## Core Premise
While being transported, the gang overpowers and slaughters their four guards. They ride hard for Shadow Ridge because Rufus once swore he would burn the town that helped put him away three years earlier. They arrive at dusk, gun down Sheriff Tom Whitaker in the street, hang his body from the hotel balcony, and declare the town theirs. They loot the bank, terrorize the saloon, and post sentries.

Word reaches Austin: if the situation is not resolved quietly within 72 hours, the governor will send two companies of Texas Rangers and declare martial law—resulting in dozens of civilian deaths and the town's ruin.

**Darin Mayweather must ride in alone, restore order, and leave the town breathing before the cavalry arrives.**

## Act 1 – The Storm Breaks (Chapters 1-6 ≈ 12,000 words)

**Chapter 1: Blood on the Trail**
The prison wagon rattles across the prairie. Flashback to the guards' overconfidence. Midnight ambush—Vance slits one throat, Rufus crushes another's skull. The gang rides free, laughing, already planning their stop at Shadow Ridge.

**Chapter 2: The Call**
Mayweather is 80 miles away finishing a stagecoach robbery case. A lathered rider brings a blood-stained telegram from the governor: "Sheriff dead. Town taken. Calhoun Gang. You alone. Seventy-two hours." Mayweather saddles his big buckskin gelding, "Justice," and rides.

**Chapter 3: Hell Comes to Shadow Ridge**
Dusk. The gang storms in. Sheriff Whitaker confronts them in the street. Rufus shoots him twice, then has the body strung up. Townsfolk are herded into the saloon at gunpoint. The bank is cracked open. Sentries are posted on the ridges and rooftops.

**Chapter 4: Rider from the South**
Mayweather arrives at moonrise. He leaves his horse in a dry wash, slips in on foot, and watches from the livery loft. He counts six outlaws, notes their positions, and sees the fear in every face he glimpses.

**Chapter 5: First Blood**
Midnight. Mayweather takes out the northern ridge sentry (Deadeye's spotter) with a knife. He leaves the body propped up with the man's own rifle across the knees—psychological warfare. He steals a stick of dynamite from the livery.

**Chapter 6: The Widow's Message**
He meets the only person willing to help quietly: Sarah Whitaker, the sheriff's widow, who runs the telegraph office. She gives him a map of the town's back alleys and tells him the gang plans to hang the mayor at noon. Mayweather promises her one thing: "They won't see sunrise."

## Act 2 – Shadow War (Chapters 7-14 ≈ 16,000 words)

**Chapter 7: Saloon Trap**
Mayweather slips into the saloon through the roof. He drops a bottle of whiskey laced with laudanum (taken from the doctor's office) near Billy the Kid. The kid drinks, passes out in the outhouse. Mayweather drags him out and locks him in the jail cell—first prisoner recovered.

**Chapter 8: Rooftop Duel**
Deadeye spots movement. A running gunfight across rooftops at dawn. Mayweather is winged in the left arm but puts a .45 slug through Deadeye's good eye.

**Chapter 9: Brothers Divided**
Rufus discovers Billy missing and starts beating Vance, accusing him of cowardice. Mayweather uses the distraction to burn the gang's horses in the livery (except two for escape later). The gang is now on foot.

**Chapter 10: The Blacksmith's Hammer**
Bull McGee hunts Mayweather through the alleys. They meet in the blacksmith shop. Mayweather uses the forge chain like a whip, then finishes Bull with his own axe handle. The townsfolk watching from windows begin to whisper hope.

**Chapter 11: Knife in the Dark**
Luis Vargas sets fire to the church to flush Mayweather out. Mayweather fights him hand-to-hand in the burning bell tower. Vargas falls through the floor into the flames.

**Chapter 12: The Mayor's Noon Hanging**
Rufus prepares to hang Mayor Jenkins in the street. Mayweather, hidden in the hotel, shoots the rope with a rifle at the exact moment the trap drops. Chaos. Vance tries to flee and is shot in the leg by Mayweather—second prisoner recovered.

**Chapter 13: The Saloon Siege**
The remaining outlaws barricade themselves in the saloon with hostages. Mayweather sends Sarah with a message under a white flag: "Walk out unarmed or I burn it down with you inside." Rufus laughs and shoots the messenger's hat off. Mayweather begins lobbing dynamite sticks through windows at carefully timed intervals—smoke and panic.

**Chapter 14: Brother Against Brother**
Vance, wounded and terrified, tries to betray Rufus for his life. Rufus kills him in the street in front of the whole town. Mayweather steps out of the smoke, face blackened, arm bleeding, and says the only line he'll speak all day: "Your brother's waiting in hell, Rufus."

## Act 3 – Reckoning and Dawn (Chapters 15-20 ≈ 12,000 words)

**Chapter 15: Main Street Showdown**
High noon. One-on-one gunfight. Rufus is faster than expected. Mayweather is hit in the side but keeps coming. Final shot: Mayweather's bullet takes Rufus between the eyes. The big man falls in the dust beside the sheriff's body.

**Chapter 16: Prisoners and Bodies**
Mayweather unlocks the jail. The two surviving gang members (Billy and the wounded Vance's corpse is dragged in) are chained. The townsfolk drag the dead outlaws to Boot Hill.

**Chapter 17: The Governor's Wire**
Sarah sends the telegram: "Town secure. Six outlaws accounted for. One marshal wounded. Send doctor and undertaker." The governor replies he will cancel the Rangers.

**Chapter 18: The Widow's Thanks**
Quiet scene at the cemetery. Sarah places flowers on her husband's fresh grave. Mayweather stands beside her, hat in hand. No words needed. She kisses his scarred cheek.

**Chapter 19: Restoring the Peace**
Mayweather helps the townsfolk clean up—righting overturned wagons, boarding broken windows, rounding up scattered cattle. The bank reopens. Children play in the street again.

**Chapter 20: Trail's End**
Dawn. Mayweather, bandaged, rides out on Justice. The whole town stands in the street watching him leave. Sarah watches longest. He tips his hat once, then disappears into the heat haze. Final line: "Shadow Ridge was free again. And somewhere down the trail, another town was already calling his name."
