# Western Validation Checklist

## Era Accuracy

- Clothing, weapons, ammunition, transport, occupations, money, law, medicine, and communication fit the year and region.
- No modern slang, idioms, institutions, tactics, or technology appear unless approved as intentional anachronism.
- Travel distance matches terrain, weather, transportation, daylight, supplies, and animal or character condition.
- Town, ranch, camp, trail, mine, homestead, or saloon details match the approved authenticity pack.

## Dialogue

- Dialogue is concise and character-specific.
- Speech is period-aware without becoming parody.
- Dialect intensity stays within project settings.
- Dialogue reveals character, advances plot, or builds tension.
- Polished modern phrasing is flagged.

## Setting

- Physical environment changes action, options, cost, danger, or mood.
- Weather, terrain, distance, animals, scarcity, tools, or social pressure influence behavior.
- Sensory details are specific and functional.
- The setting is not repeated as generic Western decoration.

## Character

- Protagonist has a clear goal, pressure, flaw, and believable choice.
- Antagonist has motive, method, pressure, and rationale.
- Supporting characters have active function or are intentionally incidental.
- Women, Native characters, settlers, minorities, and townsfolk are not treated as stereotypes or props.
- Character choices match profile, arc state, knowledge state, and continuity locks.

## Theme

- Western themes are expressed through decisions, cost, conflict, and consequence.
- Theme is not stated as a clean lesson unless the scene intentionally supports that voice.
- Violence has consequence where required by the active theme profile.
- Moral choices include real tradeoff, sacrifice, refusal, debt, or loss.

## Prose

- Prose is clear, direct, physical, and restrained.
- Emotion is shown before it is explained.
- Abstract emotional labels do not replace observable action.
- Interior thought is used deliberately, not as redundant explanation.
- Specialized Western vocabulary is natural and approved, not ornamental.

## Rollup Decision

The `WesternPrimeDirectiveValidator` may block approval if high-severity findings show that the passage is not consistent, physical, era-accurate, or character-driven.
