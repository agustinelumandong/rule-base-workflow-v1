# BookForge Short Summary

**Based on:** [BookForge Master Plan](BOOKFORGE-MASTER-PLAN.md) and [BookForge Ecosystem Evaluation](../references/ecosystem-evaluation.md)

BookForge is a human-supervised fiction production platform for planning, generating, transforming, validating, revising, reviewing, and publishing books while preserving canon, prose quality, editorial intent, and human accountability.

It supports two primary workflows:

1. **Create new books and series:** develop approved research, canon, story bibles, outlines, chapter plans, scenes, prose, revisions, and publication artifacts.
2. **Transform existing manuscripts:** preserve structure while extracting proposed canon, humanizing or editing prose, checking meaning and continuity, reviewing diffs, and approving revisions.

Its modular capabilities cover project management, canon and timelines, research with evidence, story planning, prose generation and editing, validation, narrative memory, controlled intelligence tools, human review, publishing, and operations. Every important artifact, model run, validation result, revision, and approval is versioned and traceable.

BookForge is not a chatbot, one giant prompt, or an autonomous agent system. AI models are replaceable workers operating through scoped context, declared capabilities, budgets, validation gates, and project isolation. They cannot silently change canon, approve content, bypass review, access another project, or publish a manuscript.

External tools such as retrieval, graph, research, or assistant frameworks are optional references or replaceable adapters, not product authorities. They may be adopted only for a measured need after license, security, maintenance, provenance, isolation, deterministic-testing, and benchmark review. Their outputs remain candidates until BookForge validates and approves them.

In short, BookForge turns AI-assisted writing into a controlled and reproducible editorial production pipeline:

```text
approved sources and decisions
-> structured plans and scoped context
-> model-assisted creative work
-> validation and targeted revision
-> human approval
-> traceable publication artifacts
```
