Threefingers Book 4 Outline: Burnt Trace
Working Title
Threefingers: Burnt Trace
Continuity Source
This outline continues from the end state of Threefingers: Stone Relay.

    • Jed rides south from the burned Stone Relay with the road still in his bones and no paper left from the cache.
    • Clementine rides in the same aftermath, no longer chasing Draven but not yet free of the road that brought her north.
    • Elias Draven is dead at the Stone Relay threshold.
    • Martin Vale is dead in the burning room.
    • Stone Relay is burned and cannot be used again.
    • Ruth Bell survives with her dead husband's list of names, coin, and letters.
    • Caleb Pike survives and carries a life debt he does not know how to settle.
    • Reverend Ansel Wren survives and has no clean way to walk back into ordinary preaching.
    • Nemi survives and returns toward kin with knowledge of the old crossings.
    • Hobb Lask is still alive, wounded, and unresolved.
    • The dead trader question remains open: who set that man onto Ketch's trail, and why.
Classic Western Inspiration
The story takes inspiration from the western pursuit-and-reckoning shape: a small party follows a trail of names, freight traces, old lies, and half-buried graves until one living hand behind the trouble can no longer hide behind distance. The names, setting, motives, conflicts, and ending are new to this series.
Time Period
Mid-to-late 1800s American frontier, in the same continuity as the first three Threefingers books. The outline should not depend on exact calendar dates unless the existing series bible establishes them. The world uses 1800s travel, weapons, medicine, speech, labor, and frontier social pressure.
Primary Setting
The story begins in Spur and then runs south and east through freight roads, creek fords, burned way stations, abandoned line shacks, limestone cuts, cottonwood bottoms, wagon traces, and one hidden freight yard built into broken badland stone. Towns exist at the edges, but the main pressure comes from roads between them, where old deals were made out of sight.
Setting Function
The landscape should expose hidden trade and hidden guilt. Each place should carry practical evidence: wheel ruts, ash pits, rope scars, freight hooks, cut harness, grave dirt, ledger scraps, and camp remains. Country should force the party to read work left behind. The setting is not background. It is the physical record of the business they are trying to uncover.
Tone and Style Direction
Terse, literal, grounded, and morally tense. Keep action physical and plain. Dialogue should stay short and useful. Character feeling should come through work, pursuit, silence, refusal, and hard choices about what truth to keep, what truth to expose, and what truth only brings more killing. The story should stay in the western line of the series: survival, debt, betrayal, personal justice, road knowledge, and the cost of unfinished business.
Story Pattern
This is a trail-unmasking western. Each chapter should find a new trace, expose one human link in the hidden freight line, split trust inside the traveling party, or move the pursuit closer to the one living hand who set the dead trader onto the road. The middle should feel tighter with each answer: the more the party learns, the fewer safe roads remain.
Hard Story Guardrails
    • No hidden organization controls the plot.
    • No company, bank, or civic machine secretly drives the conflict.
    • No ore-claim story.
    • No courtroom sequence.
    • No elected-lawman power structure carrying the villain.
    • The villain's power must come from personal reach, old trail knowledge, private freight dealing, and a few dangerous hands.
    • No banned plot language from the Project Lock.
    • Do not use any banned names from the current Project Lock.
    • No em-dashes in prose or outline text.
    • No dialogue tags as a stylistic habit.
    • No modern or clinical vocabulary in prose.
    • Literal western action only.
Premise
After Stone Relay burns, Ruth Bell's list becomes the last surviving paper trail to older hidden freight work tied to her dead husband, the dead trader, and the road that killed Ketch. Jed wants the paper gone before it starts another blood trail. Clementine wants the answer before another man uses ignorance as cover. Ruth wants to know which name on the list ruined her husband and sent death north. Caleb knows one name better than he admits. Wren recognizes another from years he has tried to bury.

Hobb Lask comes back into the story because he believes the list can still name money owed and freight buried. But Lask is not the true spine. The real hand behind the dead trader is Silas Creed, an old freight runner turned private broker of stolen loads, hidden drops, and quiet killings. He learned just enough of Stone Relay to send another man in first, hoping the road would cough up Ketch, Jed, or whichever survivor still held the true trail.

Book 4 is not a wagon-pressure journey like Book 3. It is a name trail western: smaller party, faster moves, fewer shelters, and a harder question at the center. If the dead trader was bait, then someone laid the bait for a reason. Book 4 answers that reason.
Book Target
Approximate length: 30,000 words Structure: 24 chapters plus epilogue Target chapter range: 1,100 to 1,350 words
Returning Characters
Jedediah "Threefingers" Thorne
Primary POV. Missing two fingers on the left hand. Right leg fracture carried from the prior books. Jed wants the paper trail buried before it restarts the road of stolen freight and dead men.
Clementine "Widow" Vesper
Primary secondary POV. Clementine stays in the story from the opening instead of riding a separate long trail. She wants the answer to the dead trader question and does not trust paper left in any hand.
Ruth Bell
Returning survivor from Book 3. Ruth carries her husband's list and letters. She begins as a protected witness and becomes an active chooser in the hunt.
Caleb Pike
Returning survivor from Book 3. Still weak from the gut wound and ashamed of his past ties to bad men. Caleb knows one name on Ruth's list from before Stone Relay and hides that fact too long.
Reverend Ansel Wren
Returning survivor from Book 3. Wren stays with the party because he knows what men like Creed do when they believe a witness can still speak.
Nemi
Returns briefly in the opening Spur aftermath and later through route knowledge carried forward from his kin. He does not become the main guide this time, but his old-road knowledge helps shape one turn in the pursuit.
Hobb Lask
Returning road gunman. Wounded, angry, and still hunting Ruth's list for his own reasons. He is not the hidden author of the dead trader problem, but he remains a live danger across the first two acts.
New Characters
Silas Creed
Primary antagonist. Former freight runner, now a private broker of stolen loads, hidden drop points, and hired trail violence. Creed is patient, practical, and careful about using other men as probes before he risks himself. He sent the dead trader north with false confidence and partial knowledge to flush out anyone tied to Stone Relay.
Del Merrin
Creed's nearest hand. Wagon breaker, rope man, and enforcer. Not a mastermind. Dangerous because he does not hesitate.
Lila Quoin
Roadhouse widow who once handled meals, beds, and quiet room rentals for freight hands moving between Spur and the south road. She knows names she wishes she did not know.
Orris Pike
No blood kin to Caleb. Mule trader and liar with one foot in every bad bargain. He sold the dead trader the first false piece of the trail and now fears Creed as much as Jed does.
Boone Kester
The dead trader's older brother. Teamster, not fighter. He enters the story when he learns his brother did not die in random road trouble. His grief makes the answer harder to leave buried.
Core Conflicts
    • Jed wants Ruth's list destroyed before it starts a second hidden freight war.
    • Clementine wants the dead trader question answered before the last proof disappears.
    • Ruth wants to know which living man used her husband and the dead trader as road meat.
    • Caleb hides prior contact with one of the names on Ruth's list.
    • Wren knows one old killing tied to Creed and has delayed speaking it aloud.
    • Hobb Lask wants the list for personal leverage and missing coin.
    • Silas Creed wants every surviving paper piece back and every surviving witness silenced.
Act Structure
Act 1: The Names Leave Spur
The Stone Relay survivors reach Spur, bury the dead they can, and discover that Ruth's list ties back toward the dead trader rather than away from him. Hobb Lask closes in. One living name on the list points to Silas Creed.
Act 2: The Hidden Freight Trail
Jed, Clementine, Ruth, Caleb, and Wren follow the line through road houses, graves, quiet camps, and freight scars. Each stop gives one more human link in the chain. Hobb Lask keeps colliding with the trail for his own reasons until he breaks against the larger truth.
Act 3: Ash Ledger
The party reaches a hidden freight yard where Creed has stored goods, kept private records, and buried the last clean answer to the dead trader question. The truth comes out under gun pressure. Jed and Clementine must decide whether to expose the remaining paper or burn it as they burned Stone Relay.
Chapter Outline

Chapter 1: Spur Smoke
Word Target: ~1,150 words

The wagon creaks into Spur with Marrow's body still in back and Stone Relay smoke still on their coats. Ruth refuses to surrender the box. Jed wants the paper gone before town eyes settle on it.


Chapter 2: Marrow Buried
Word Target: ~1,150 words

Marrow is buried above Spur while Boone Kester arrives looking for the truth about his dead brother. Ruth finds one name in her husband's list that matches something Boone carries from the dead trader's effects.


Chapter 3: Lask in Spur
Word Target: ~1,150 words

Hobb Lask rides into Spur with his wound bound and two new hands behind him. He offers Ruth one last trade for the box. Jed refuses for her, and the first town fight sends Lask back out to wait on the road.


Chapter 4: Caleb Knows the Name
Word Target: ~1,150 words

Caleb recognizes the name Silas Creed in Ruth's papers and tries to keep quiet. Clementine sees the lie in his face before Jed does. Caleb admits he once heard Draven and Creed named in the same camp month apart.


Chapter 5: South Fork Grave
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Boone Kester leads the party to the place where his brother's body was first found. Jed reads wagon traces, second-horse sign, and a false camp shape that proves the dead trader was guided, not lost.


Chapter 6: Wren Speaks Late
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Wren admits he knew Creed years earlier under another freight job that ended with a driver dead and the books burned. He kept silent because he never proved it. Now the same habits show in the road sign.


Chapter 7: The First Ledger Scrap
Word Target: ~1,250 words

At a ruined cook shack south of Spur, Ruth finds a half-burned ledger leaf hidden under floor planks. The writing ties her husband's dealings to Creed's freight marks and to the same season the dead trader vanished.


Chapter 8: Lila Quoin's House
Word Target: ~1,250 words

The party reaches Lila Quoin's road house. She confirms Creed used to rent back rooms, weigh coin in private, and send strangers out with partial directions they never fully understood.


Chapter 9: Lask Follows Wrong
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Lask hits the road house after the party leaves and learns too little too late. Clementine reads the damage behind him and understands he is still hunting the list, not the deeper truth.


Chapter 10: Dead Man's Harness
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Jed and Boone find the dead trader's cut harness hanging in a cottonwood break where someone meant the road to forget it. Boone nearly loses himself there. Jed sees that grief can turn a witness into another problem if left idle.


Chapter 11: Orris Pike Lies First
Word Target: ~1,250 words

The party corners Orris Pike, who sold the dead trader a false start on the north road. Orris claims he thought the buyer only wanted buried freight. Clementine forces him far enough to reveal Creed paid in advance and ordered silence after.


Chapter 12: Camp Without Fire
Word Target: ~1,250 words

A no-fire camp in open stone country strains the party. Ruth wants the answer before the papers burn. Jed wants the papers burned before the answer spreads. Caleb wavers between them.


Chapter 13: Creed's Rope Man
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Del Merrin reaches the party at dawn with a rifle from high stone and a warning from Creed: hand over the box and keep breathing. Wren breaks the pressure with one long shot, but Merrin escapes after taking Boone's mule.


Chapter 14: Nemi's Turn Marker
Word Target: ~1,250 words

A message from Nemi's kin reaches the party at a ferry settlement: one old crossing south of the limestone cuts still avoids the main freight trace. Jed uses the route to stay ahead of Lask and force Creed to guess.


Chapter 15: The Quiet Load
Word Target: ~1,250 words

The old crossing leads to a hidden unloading place where freight hooks, axle grease, and tarp scraps prove Creed kept moving stolen loads long after Stone Relay stopped being safe. Ruth understands her husband was never a partner, only a useful clerk.


Chapter 16: Lask Makes His Bargain
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Lask catches the party at a dry arroyo and offers to help kill Creed in exchange for the list and coin afterward. No one trusts him. Jed lets him ride away alive because Creed is still the truer mark.


Chapter 17: Boone Takes the Truth Hard
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Boone learns his brother was sent north with false courage, false directions, and just enough paper to attract Ketch if the road favored it. The dead trader was bait from the start. Boone nearly rides off to die alone after hearing it.


Chapter 18: Caleb Pays Forward
Word Target: ~1,250 words

Caleb finally admits he once carried one sealed note for Creed without knowing the full work. The admission almost breaks Ruth from the party. Clementine keeps the group from splitting because the yard is too close now.


Chapter 19: Merrin at the Lime Cut
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Del Merrin and two hands ambush the party in a limestone cut. Caleb saves Wren at close range and takes a fresh wound for it. Merrin dies, but not before confirming Creed has already moved the freight books to the hidden yard.


Chapter 20: Lask Breaks Wrong
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Lask reaches Creed first and tries to take coin and leverage at gunpoint. Creed answers with steel and leaves Lask dying short of the yard. Jed's party finds him in time to hear the last useful piece: the yard lies under broken red rock beyond a dry freight road.


Chapter 21: Red Rock Yard
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Jed leads the party into the badland freight yard by a side wash instead of the main trace. They find hidden wagons, tarped goods, and one locked office cut into stone. The place is more organized than a thief camp and still fully personal in its control.


Chapter 22: Creed's Answer
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Creed comes out talking because he still believes truth can be priced. He admits he sent the dead trader north with partial route talk, hoping either Ketch or any survivor tied to Stone Relay would show themselves by trying to stop him.


Chapter 23: Burnt Trace
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Inside the stone office, Ruth finds the true ledger naming freight drops, cuts, bought lies, and the payment made for the dead trader's bait run. Jed wants the books burned. Boone wants them carried to every town touched by the freight line.


Chapter 24: One Hand Left
Word Target: ~1,350 words

Creed tries to leave through the wash with the ledger and Ruth at gunpoint. Clementine cuts off the escape line. Jed kills Creed in the yard after the ledger tears free and the tarped loads catch fire. Boone gets his answer, but not a clean peace from it.


Epilogue: South from Red Rock
Word Target: ~1,150 words

At first light the yard burns down to iron, ash, and split wagon wood. Boone carries one copied page and the truth of his brother's death. Ruth keeps one name for herself and lets the rest burn. Jed and Clementine ride out together longer than they did after Stone Relay, though neither calls it a promise.
Ending State for Book 5
    • Silas Creed is dead.
    • Hobb Lask is dead.
    • The dead trader question is answered: Creed used him as bait with false route knowledge to flush out Stone Relay's surviving link.
    • Ruth Bell survives and chooses what parts of the ledger truth stay alive.
    • Caleb Pike survives another wound and earns back a little standing through action instead of pleading.
    • Boone Kester survives with a partial answer and one copied page, enough to name blame without carrying the whole hidden freight trail forward.
    • The hidden freight yard is burned.
    • Jed and Clementine end the book less divided than before, but still without easy peace.
    • Book 4 resolves the dead trader mystery while leaving one smaller future thread alive: a copied page and one unburned name may still matter if the series needs another road.
