# Chapter 4 Continuity Out

## Characters
- Jake Moses: Still 14, surviving winter as a captive laborer. Hands are cracked, rawhide cuts are closing, and he has built a better bark shelter.
- Thorn Blackroot: Practical elder mentor. Saves Jake from freezing through fire, food, shelter correction, and short instruction without adoption or ceremony.

## Locations
- Mountain village: Winter has settled over the work grounds, shelters, fires, creek path, and hide racks.
- Jake's bark shelter: Rebuilt low against the wind with overlapped bark, pine bough bedding, packed base, and drainage trench.
- Thorn's instruction fire: Small fire used for lessons in coals, smoke, dry wood, and food preservation.

## Changes
- Jake nearly freezes and learns stubbornness alone will not keep him alive.
- Thorn begins practical mentorship through fire care, bark placement, dry wood selection, and food smoking.
- Jake uses farm knowledge of posts, slope, knots, and drainage to build a winter shelter that holds.
- Jake learns early food preservation and fire maintenance: smoke keeps meat, banked coals can live overnight, wet wood must be shaved to dry heart.
- Mentorship is established without changing Jake's captive status.

## Human Stakes Carried
- Jake Moses: Carries the pressure to learn survival skills because cold and hunger can kill him faster than open violence.
- Jake Moses: Carries the cost of remaining a captive while depending on Thorn's hard instruction to live.
- Jake Moses: Carries the pressure to turn farm knowledge into wilderness skill before winter breaks him.
- Thorn Blackroot: Carries the pressure of teaching Jake only what effort earns, without softening village hierarchy.

## Unresolved Pressure
- Jake remains a captive in the village with no confirmed news of his family.
- Thorn's mentorship is practical but unsentimental, and Jake must earn each lesson through work.
- Village status has not changed; Jake is still mocked and used for labor.

## Next Chapter Must Know
- Chapter 5 can assume mentorship is established but not affectionate or ceremonial.
- Jake knows basic bark shelter construction, fire banking, dry wood selection, and smoke preservation.
- Jake has decided to learn aggressively as a survival choice.
- The bark-shelter knowledge should pay forward to later construction skills.
