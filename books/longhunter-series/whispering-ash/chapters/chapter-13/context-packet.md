# Chapter 13: The Gorge ## Scene 1 Jake found the gorge by following active game trails. Deer tracks crossed a muddy bar below a bend in the river, then climbed into a cleft where spruce and stone narrowed the light. He followed from downwind with the ash cloak close around him. The river shouldered through rock below. Above it, a sweet spring came out from under a moss shelf and ran clear over gravel. He drank there first. Then he looked like a man choosing ground to live on. The gorge had one narrow mouth from the river, one steep back climb, and stone shoulders on both sides. A man coming in would show himself before reaching the flat. Game trails crossed high and low. Spruce stood thick enough for logs. Wind moved over the top but left the lower shelf still. No neighbors. No town law. No hidden settlement smoke. Jake walked the perimeter twice, claiming nothing with words and everything with basic fortification work. He marked a cache hollow under a boulder, a place for a meat pole, a flat shelf for a cabin, and a lower patch where a small barn might stand if he ever kept a horse or mule.
