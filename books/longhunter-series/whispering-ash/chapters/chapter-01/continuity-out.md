- Mother and sisters: Last seen near the creek brush during the raid. Their exact fate remains unknown from Jake's limited POV.
- Father and brothers: Away hunting before the raid begins. Their exact fate remains unknown from Jake's limited POV.
- Red-clay warrior: Killed by Jake with the family farm axe near the woodpile.
- Scarred warrior: Alive, participates in Jake's capture, and marches with the raiding party.

## Locations
- Family farm in western Virginia: Cabin, barn, yard, rails, and stored goods are burned or plundered during the raid.
- Cornfield and creek brush: Last place Jake sees his mother and sisters before he is forced away.
- Forest trail: Leads uphill from the burned homestead toward the Appalachian Mountains.

## Changes
- Jake is no longer a free farm boy; he is a bound captive being marched west.
- Jake has killed a warrior with a farm axe in desperate defense of the homestead.
- The Moses homestead is destroyed by fire.
- Jake's family is separated from him, with no confirmed reunion or confirmed deaths in this chapter.

## Human Stakes Carried
- Jake Moses: Carries the pressure of farm memory, his mother's field orders, his sisters at the creek, and his father and brothers gone hunting.
- Jake Moses: Carries the cost of the unresolved creek-brush sight and burning cabin into the forced march.
- Jake Moses: Faces immediate pressure because the raiding party now knows the captive boy has killed one of their own.

## Unresolved Pressure

- The family outcome remains unknown from Jake's POV.
- The raiding party may treat Jake as more than plunder because he killed one of their warriors.
