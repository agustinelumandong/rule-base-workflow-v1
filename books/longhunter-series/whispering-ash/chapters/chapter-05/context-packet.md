# Chapter 5: The Hunt ## Scene 1 Two winters and two summers changed Jake without asking leave. His trousers rose above his ankles and had to be cut and pieced with hide. Loads of wood widened his shoulders. Hunger, cold, smoke, and work took the last roundness from his face. The rawhide scars at his wrists faded to pale cords, but they never vanished. The village still called him Whispering Ash. The name came less often with laughter now. It came as a summons to work. It came as warning. Children used it because they had never known another name for him. Jake did not answer quickly to it at first. After a while, not answering brought a blow or extra labor. He remained apart. Thorn Blackroot did not change that. The old man taught him knots, bark, coals, snares, dry wood, snow sign, and the difference between noise and sound. He taught by doing, by correcting, by taking a task from Jake's hands and making him do it again. Praise was a grunt if the work held. Failure was a stick tapped against the mistake. Jake learned to take both the same way. In the second spring, Thorn took him beyond the creek and made him walk on wet leaves without turning them over. Jake failed before sunrise.
