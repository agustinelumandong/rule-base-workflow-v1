# Chapter 5 Continuity Out

## Characters
- Jake Moses: Older, taller, leaner, and more skilled after years two to four. He has helped turn and dress a deer and returned carrying meat.
- Thorn Blackroot: Continues as practical mentor. Allows Jake onto the hunt and gives restrained approval after Jake proves useful.

## Locations
- Mountain village: Jake's camp work begins shifting from the lowest labor toward useful snare and hunting support work.
- Mountain hunting grounds: Ridge, seep, laurel, hardwood opening, deer trail, and kill site.
- Village return clearing: Meat is delivered and Jake receives limited recognition.

## Changes
- Time has advanced through years two to four of captivity.
- Jake has grown physically leaner and stronger through work, hunger, and Thorn's training.
- Jake joins a hunting party despite skepticism.
- Jake reads wind and cover, helps turn a buck into the hunters' shot, and helps field dress the deer.
- Jake receives first restrained praise from Thorn and is treated less like a slave, though not equal.

## Human Stakes Carried
- Jake Moses: Carries the pressure to keep proving usefulness because partial respect can disappear if he fails.
- Jake Moses: Carries the cost of belonging only through labor and skill, not freedom or full acceptance.
- Jake Moses: Carries the pressure to remember deer sign, wind, and field dressing for later survival alone.
- Thorn Blackroot: Carries the pressure of having vouched for Jake enough to bring him into a hunting party.

## Unresolved Pressure
- Jake remains an outsider and captive, not a full member of the village.
- The name Whispering Ash has less bite, but Jake has not reclaimed it as his own.
- Jake's family fate remains unresolved.
