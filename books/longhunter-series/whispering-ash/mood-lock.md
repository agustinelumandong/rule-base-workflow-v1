# Mood Lock - Whispering Ash (Longhunter Series Book One)

## Genre and Atmosphere
- **Genre:** Grounded Historical Western / Frontier Survival
- **Atmosphere:** Gritty, silent, vast, and cold. The narrative emphasizes the overwhelming scale of the Appalachian wilderness, the harshness of winter, and the physical reality of isolation.

## Historical and Time-Period Assumptions
- **Time Period:** 1780 through 1788 (the late Revolutionary War and post-war frontier era).
- **Technology and Material Culture:** Flintlock long rifles, black powder, lead balls, patch knives, trade muskets, wood axes, homespun wool, buckskins, hide scraping, bark shelters, spruce log construction.
- **Cultural Dynamics:** Direct clashes and parallel tracking methods between Cherokee hunting bands and white frontier scouts. Swapping of trail knowledge is practical, cautious, and rooted in survival.

## Prose Style Constraints
- **Perspective:** Strict third-person limited POV from Jake Moses. The reader only sees, hears, and knows what Jake physically experiences.
- **Register:** Terse, literal, and concrete. Focus on sensory details: the smell of damp soil, the weight of a wet buckskin cloak, the bite of frost on fingers, the drag of a raw hide scraper.
- **Rhythm:** Medium to long weathered sentences for travel and description. Short, punchy sentences for action and combat. Avoid modern thriller fragment loops.
- **Emotion:** Shown through work, micro-behaviors, and silence. No internal monologues detailing emotional states. Do not explain why Jake feels a certain way; describe his actions, his breathing, or his labor.

## Vocabulary Direction
- **Avoid (Modern / Clinical / Legalese):** tactical position, leverage, pressure point, emotional cost, control the ground, problem, situation, objective, threat, move fast, stay focused, realized, processed, understood, psychological, emotional.
- **Allowed Frontier Flavor:** reckon, ought, yonder, lit out, fetched, struck north, made camp, hard country, took sick, played out, by sundown, come morning.

## Dialogue Direction
- **Approach:** Rugged, simple, and sparse. Characters speak only when necessary.
- **Form:** Avoid poetic or theatrical phrasing. Dialogue must remain functional and grounded in immediate physical needs. Use contractions and simple sentence structures.
- **Tags:** Carry conversations through action and tone rather than repetitive dialogue tags.

## Violence and Action Direction
- **Approach:** Grounded, messy, and physically taxing. Combat has immediate physical consequences: exhaustion, muscle strain, blood loss, and lingering pain.
- **Realism:** Focus on the mechanical difficulty of using period weapons: reloading a flintlock, drawing a heavy bow, the weight of a farm axe or tomahawk.
- **Consequence:** Highlight the physical reality of killing: the weight of a body, the smell of blood, and the quiet aftermath.

## Avoidance List
- Do not use em-dashes anywhere in the manuscript or planning files.
- Do not explain character motivations or subtext.
- Do not skip travel transitions abruptly. Show the weariness of the journey.
- Do not use modern or cinematic action-movie dialogue.
