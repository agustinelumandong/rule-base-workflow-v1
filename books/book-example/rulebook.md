If a lower source conflicts with a higher source, follow the higher source.

## Length Handling Rules

- Treat the about 30,000-word guidance as book-level planning only.
- Do not force fixed chapter or beat lengths.
- Expand from source-supported actions, scenes, consequences, dialogue pressure, and continuity.

## Do Not Invent

- Do not invent named characters, locations, motives, or outcomes not present in source.
- Do not resolve `UNKNOWN` values by adding unsupported facts.

## Characters

## Chapter Continuity Ledger

### Chapter 1: [Title]

- **Required Story Movement:** Establish setup and first conflict from chapter source.
- **Continuity Out:** Introduce core pressures and visible forward state.

### Chapter 2: [Title]

- **Required Story Movement:** Advance conflict toward second-tier pressure.
- **Continuity Out:** Carry unresolved pressure into Chapter 3.

### Chapter 3: [Title]

- **Required Story Movement:** Resolve current chapter arc and establish transition state.
- **Continuity Out:** Carry final pressure into the epilogue.

### Epilogue

- **Required Story Movement:** Close immediate arc and preserve forward setup for next work.
- **Continuity Out:** Leave only source-supported setup for continuation.

## Unknowns

- Specific character names in unresolved passages.
- Exact timeline details for unresolved transitions.
- Any unspecified motives not explicitly present in source.
